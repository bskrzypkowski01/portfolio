# Portfolio – Błażej Skrzypkowski

Power electronics engineer and master's student at the Faculty of Electrical Engineering, Warsaw University of Technology. Specializing in power converters, digital control systems and microcontroller programming.

## Projects

### 🏆 [IFEC 2025](./IFEC%202025)
Winner of the International Future Energy Challenge (IFEC) 2025 — a prestigious global competition for students organized under the auspices of IEEE, the world's largest organization of technical professionals, focused on innovations in power electronics.

Last year's challenge was to design and build a high-efficiency isolated DC-DC converter suitable for AI data center power supply applications.

Our team passed the selection stage, competed in the semifinals at APEC conference in Atlanta, USA (March 2025), advanced to the final held in Taiwan (July 2025), and was named the best team in the world among the top 8 finalists.

### 📘 [Master Thesis](./Master%20Thesis)
Bridgeless Totem-Pole PFC converter with GaN transistors and digital control based on STM32H7. Designed and built entirely from scratch — starting from simulations, through PCB design, firmware development on STM32, all the way to a custom CAN-based oscilloscope application written with AI assistance for real-time signal monitoring and live parameter tuning.

### 📗 [Engineering Thesis](./Engineering%20Thesis)
Design and construction of a full-bridge converter with GaN E-HEMT (Enhancement mode High Electron Mobility Transistor) transistors. The project covers GaN technology analysis, PCB design, gate driver and thermal circuit design. Converter was tested in two operating modes: continuous operation with inductive load and inverter operation with resistive load. 

### 🔬 [DREN Student Research Group](./DREN%20Student%20Research%20Group)
"Drones & Renewables" (DREN) is a student research club at Warsaw University of Technology focused on power electronics and electronic systems.

As president of the club, I led the team in building an autonomous drone charging station powered by photovoltaic panels with energy storage.

The club also competed in IFEC 2025, becoming the first Polish team in history to win the competition.
